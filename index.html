Political Science

I study how social divisions shape political outcomes.

I am a Political Science student based in Amsterdam, with a focus on comparative politics, quantitative analysis, and policy-oriented research. My work explores how internal political cleavages influence institutions, public debate, and foreign policy.

I am especially interested in the point where domestic politics stops looking domestic.

What this site is for

This website brings together my academic work, research interests, and professional profile. It is meant as a space to present current projects, selected writing, and the questions that shape my approach to politics and policy.

I am particularly drawn to political analysis that links structure and agency: how broad social divisions shape party systems, and how those same divisions reappear in decisions that are often presented as purely strategic or external.

Selected focus

Thesis

Foreign policy and cleavage theory

My thesis examines whether party positions on foreign policy in Mexico and Colombia reflect domestic social cleavages rather than purely leader-driven choices.

Research

Policy-oriented work

I have contributed to research that translates political analysis into practical recommendations, particularly in democratic resilience and governance.

Approach

Analytical but communicative

I am interested not only in producing analysis, but in making it clear, usable, and connected to real institutions and policy debates.
